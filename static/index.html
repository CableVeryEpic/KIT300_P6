📖 How to Read the IPA Chart

This chart represents the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA), a system for transcribing speech sounds across languages. Click a symbol to hear it.

  • Consonants (Pulmonic): Made with air from the lungs. Arranged by how and where the sound is produced.
  • Vowels: Organized by tongue height (e.g., "close" vs "open") and position (front, central, back).
  • Non-Pulmonic Consonants: Special sounds like clicks, ejectives, and implosives used in certain languages.
  • Affricates: Begin like a stop and release like a fricative (e.g., t͡ʃ in "church").
  • Other Symbols: Rare or specialized sounds used in advanced phonetic contexts.
